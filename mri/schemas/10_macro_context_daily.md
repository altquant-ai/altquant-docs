# Macro Market Context (Daily)

High-level macroeconomic backdrop for risk assets. This covers interest rates, yield curve structure, real yields, and systemic liquidity conditions. It provides the financial conditions context that influences all equity positioning.

## What This Answers

What is the macro backdrop for risk assets?

## Availability

This view is available in Core and Premium tiers only.

## Data Structure

One row per trading day. Primary key is market_date.

## What You Get

The view provides interest rate levels for short-term and long-term Treasuries, term structure slope showing yield curve shape (normal versus inverted), real yields measuring inflation-adjusted returns on safe assets, a systemic liquidity index for financial conditions, liquidity regime classification, rates regime classification, credit conditions through a stress subindex, and momentum indicators showing direction of yields and credit conditions.

## Liquidity Regimes

**ACCOMMODATIVE** — Easy financial conditions. Supportive environment for risk assets.

**NEUTRAL** — Normal financial conditions.

**TIGHTENING** — Tight financial conditions. Creates headwinds for risk assets.

## Rates Regimes

**LOW** — Below-average interest rate environment.

**NORMAL** — Average interest rate environment.

**HIGH** — Above-average interest rate environment.

## How Teams Use This

Asset allocation models incorporate these inputs to adjust across asset classes. Macro overlay strategies use liquidity and rates regime to inform equity exposure. Duration-sensitive and rate-sensitive portfolios use the rates context for positioning. Cross-asset risk systems integrate this as a foundational input.
